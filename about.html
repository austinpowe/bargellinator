Bargellinator is a generative* bargello* pattern designer.

*Generative here means that we're giving computers a small set of constrained inputs and seeing what it kicks out at the end!

*Bargello is an embroidery technique that uses yarn on canvas mesh.

Each time you reload the page, you get a never-before-seen bargello tapestry.

Bargello is a 17th century needlework technique that uses brightly colored yarn on canvas or plastic mesh to create repetitive patterns.

The technique had a resurgence in popularity in the 1960s, but has fallen out of favor in the intervening decades. Because of bargello's waning popularity, there simply aren't as many patterns available to the modern stitcher as there are for other fiber arts, like knitting, cross-stitch, embroidery, or weaving.

This site my personal attempt to decode what makes bargello patterns so fun and unique, while hopefully providing inspiration to other stitchers. (It's a remarkably mathematical art form!
